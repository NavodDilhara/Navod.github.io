Why The Witcher 3 Wild Hunt is Still Better Than Most Games in 2024

Hey everyone! Today, I want to talk about my all-time favorite game, "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt." Even though it was released back in 2015, I still think it's better than most of the games out there in 2024. Let me tell you why. Storytelling at Its Finest First and foremost, "The Witcher 3" excels in storytelling. The narrative is rich, immersive, and incredibly well-crafted. You play as Geralt of Rivia, a monster hunter on a quest to find his adopted daughter, Ciri, who is being pursued by the Wild Hunt. The main story is gripping, but what really sets this game apart are the side quests. Each side quest feels like its own mini-story, with deep characters and meaningful choices that can impact the game world. This level of storytelling is something you rarely see in games, even today.

Complex Characters The characters in "The Witcher 3" are some of the best-written in gaming history. Geralt himself is a complex protagonist with a dry sense of humor and a strong moral code. Then you have characters like Yennefer, Triss, and Ciri, who are not just side characters but integral parts of the story with their own arcs and development. Even the NPCs you meet on your journey feel like real people with their own stories and motivations.

Vast and Beautiful Open World The game's open world is vast, diverse, and stunningly beautiful. From the war-torn lands of Velen to the bustling streets of Novigrad, and the serene isles of Skellige, every location is meticulously crafted with attention to detail. The world feels alive, with dynamic weather, day-night cycles, and NPCs going about their daily routines. Exploring this world is a joy in itself, and even after all these years, it still holds up visually against modern games.

Deep and Rewarding Combat System The combat system in "The Witcher 3" is deep and rewarding. It's not just about hacking and slashing; you need to think strategically. Geralt has a range of abilities at his disposal, from swordplay to alchemy and magic signs. Each encounter, whether it's against humans or monsters, requires a different approach. You need to prepare, study your enemies, and choose the right tactics and equipment. This depth adds a layer of immersion that many modern games lack.

Music and Atmospherek  "The Witcher 3" is nothing short of phenomenal. The music, composed by Marcin Przybyłowicz, sets the tone perfectly, whether you're in a tense battle or exploring a peaceful village. Combined with the atmospheric sounds and stunning visuals, the game creates an immersive experience that pulls you into its world like few others can.

Free DLCs and Expansions  of "The Witcher 3," have set a high bar for post-launch support. They released 16 free DLCs, adding new quests, gear, and features to the game. On top of that, the two major expansions, "Hearts of Stone" and "Blood and Wine," are practically full games in themselves, offering even more content and stories to dive into. This kind of support and respect for the players is rare in today's gaming industry.

In conclusion, "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt" stands the test of time and remains a benchmark for quality in the gaming industry. Its masterful storytelling, complex characters, beautiful open world, deep combat system, and exceptional post-launch support make it better than most games even in 2024. If you haven't played it yet, do yourself a favor and dive into the world of The Witcher. You won't regret it.

Navod Dilhara
